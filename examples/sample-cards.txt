q: DNS: what does an A record map?
a: hostname → IPv4 address

q: DNS: record type that maps a hostname to an IPv6 address?
a: AAAA

q: DNS: why can a stale record keep resolving long after you changed it?
a: resolvers serve their cache until the TTL expires

q: DNS: CNAME vs A — why can't you use CNAME at a zone apex?
a: CNAME can't coexist with the apex's SOA/NS records

q: prod incident: users still get the old IP after a DNS change — first thing to check?
a: TTL / resolver caches

q: DNS: default port?
a: 53 (UDP and TCP)

q: DNS: classic mistake right before migrating a record?
a: not lowering the TTL in advance

q: DNS: "www.example.com → example.github.io → 185.199.108.153" — what record chain is this?
a: CNAME, then A

q: DNS: the [...] record lists the mail servers that accept mail for a domain
a: MX

q: DNS: why does recursive resolution start at the root servers?
a: it's a delegation hierarchy — root → TLD → authoritative

q: DNS: classic size limit of a UDP DNS message?
a: ~512 bytes (EDNS0 raises it)

q: DNS: what problem does DNSSEC solve, and where do the details live?
a: authenticating that records weren't forged (spoofing/cache poisoning) — details: RFC 4033+ / your resolver docs

q: DNS: which record type forbids other records at the same name?
a: CNAME
a1: TXT
a2: MX
a3: AAAA
