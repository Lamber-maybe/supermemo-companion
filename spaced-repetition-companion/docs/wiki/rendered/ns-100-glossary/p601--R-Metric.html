Glossary:R-Metric

R-Metric (Recall Metric)
Absolute measure of spaced repetition algorithm performance based on their ability to predict recall before a grade is scored. As of SuperMemo 20, R-Metric compares Algorithm SM-19 against an optimally weighted average of predictions from SM-2, SM-15, SM-19, SM-20, and FSRS. The weighting is determined by Algorithm Arena, where more accurate algorithms gain weight. R-Metric is shown as a percentage in Toolkit : Statistics : Analysis : Use : Efficiency : R-Metric. It is the difference between the performance of the two competing predictions: R-Metric=LSRM(Alg-19)-LSRM(Arena), where LSRM is the least squares predicted recall measure. R-Metric less than zero shows the superiority of Algorithm SM-19. R-Metric greater than zero indicates the superiority of the weighted algorithm blend. LSRM is the square root of the average of squared absolute differences in recall predictions: abs(Recall-PredictedRecall), where Recall is 0 for failing grades and 1 for passing grades. PredictedRecall is the prediction issued by the algorithm before the repetition. Expect a negative metric before Algorithm Arena determines how well individual algorithms perform in the collection.
