Glossary:Final drill

final drill
last (optional) stage of the learning process on a given day, in which only items that scored a grade less than Good (4) are repeated, as long as they continue scoring below Good. On the next day, the final drill queue is again shifted to the end of the learning day.
The final drill stage is not executed if Toolkit : Options : Learning : Skip final drill is checked.
To delete the queue, use Learn : Cut drills from the main menu. If the final drill exceeds 500 items and is older than 10 days, SuperMemo will propose its deletion.
Important: Since October 2024, the final drill no longer follows a strict sequential order. To facilitate longer drills on particularly difficult material, some elements in the queue are randomly prioritized for review. This approach helps reinforce connections between concepts and improves the chances of completing the drill, even when certain portions remain challenging. If the randomization makes the drill feel too easy, you can assign a failing grade without negative consequences. This change facilitates cramming for a deadline at school. For details, see here.
As of SuperMemo 20, active final drill ensures that repetitions on successive days are registered in the repetition history to minimize interference with the SuperMemo Algorithm.
