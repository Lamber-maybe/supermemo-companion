Learning with SuperMemo is based on repetition (see why).

The button Learn (bottom-left in the picture) makes it possible to learn the material stored in SuperMemo.

Repetition cycle

[edit | edit source]

When you press Learn, SuperMemo will ask you to make repetitions (i.e. review due material). In each repetition you will do the following:

  1. Look at the question and try to answer it (mentally or verbally)
  2. Choose Show answer (at the bottom of the screen) to see the correct answer
  3. Compare your response (from Step 1) with the answer displayed on the screen (in Step 2)
  4. Choose a grade that will reflect the accuracy of your response (e.g. Good, Fail, etc.) (see: grade descriptions)
  5. Choose Next repetition to continue (and go back to Step 1)

The repetition cycle may be shorter. If the repetition is passive (e.g. reading an article), you may immediately jump to the Next repetition stage.

Remember:

  • Your first repetitions will usually come up only in 3-5 days after you first add new material. Before they are due, you will only see "Nothing more to learn". This is normal. If Learn does not ask any questions, use Add new to add more material
  • Do not look at the timer, the response time is ignored when measuring your performance.
  • If you keep cycling through items and you never see "Nothing more to learn", you are in the final drill stage and you must start giving passing grades (Good or Great)

Grades

[edit | edit source]

The grades are defined as follows (keyboard shortcuts in parentheses):

REMEMBERING FORGETTING
Great (press 5)
excellent response
Good (press 4)
correct response provided with some hesitation
Pass (press 3)
answer recalled with difficulty; perhaps, slightly incorrect
Fail (press 2)
wrong response that makes you say I knew it!
Bad (press 1)
wrong response; the correct answer seems to be familiar
Null (press 0)
complete blackout; you do not even recall ever knowing the answer.
If you stick to the rules of SuperMemo, you should rarely or never need this grade. This is why it is not displayed on the standard grade panel. To issue that grade, use the keyboard.

Figure: As of SuperMemo 18, grades are illustrated with vivid emojis by default. If you prefer the old text-based grades (i.e. Great, Pass, Bad, etc.), uncheck Grade icons in Toolkit : Options : SuperMemo.

Grading tips

[edit | edit source]
  • Important! You only have to understand the difference between Pass and Fail!
    • Pass and more means remembering!
    • Fail and less means forgetting!
  • your grading will work as long as you always grade yourself consistently and clearly differentiate between Pass and Fail. Over time, you will develop strong habits that will help you select grades automatically without much thinking
  • instead of clicking a grade button you can just press a keyboard shortcut (e.g. 5 to choose Great or 2 to choose Fail)

Regular repetitions are the key to success with SuperMemo

[edit | edit source]

In SuperMemo you first memorize the material and then review it when the program asks you to do so.

You memorize new material at your own pace but once it is memorized, you must make repetitions exactly as demanded by SuperMemo. This is not a limitation of the program. This is a limitation of human memory!

The power of SuperMemo is in providing the best timing for repetitions. Optimally, you should work with SuperMemo every day! This way you will not accumulate outstanding material and you will not deviate from the optimum schedule of repetitions!

Your repetitions should proceed daily until the following equivalent conditions are met:

  • you see the message: Nothing more to learn
  • Outstanding equals to 0+0 in the Statistics window (or simply 0)
  • the second field of the status bar displays (0+0)/x, for example: (0+0)/23 (or simply 0)
  • the progress bar on the status bar becomes blue (or is inactive)

Learning stages

[edit | edit source]

Each day, your learning will proceed in three stages:

  1. Repetitions - reviewing material that has been memorized earlier (to make sure you do not forget it)
  2. Memorizing - if you have time, you can memorize new material by answering Yes to Do you want to learn new material? (this message is suppressed until you memorize your first 100 elements)
  3. Final drill - reviewing material that caused most problems in the first two stages of learning (Skip final drill must be unchecked in Toolkit : Options : Learning)

Incremental learning

[edit | edit source]

Once you master the above basic learning process, you should consider learning about incremental learning.

The main differences between classical SuperMemo, as described above, and incremental learning:

  • less typing, higher learning speed
  • reading new articles or watching new videos may be interleaved with standard repetitions (as described above)
  • the role of Memorizing and Final drill stages is diminished. All material is memorized by default and your work is more mnemonic. This requires far less drilling
  • with the help of the priority queue, you can add far more material to your collection than you can possibly review. This is why, in incremental reading, you will often still have lots of lower priority outstanding material in the queue at the end of the day

As incremental learning is rather complex, you should first sense the power of SuperMemo by mastering the basic learning process as described on this page.

使用 SuperMemo 进行学习是基于重复（参见为什么）。

按钮学习（图中左下角）可以学习 SuperMemo 中存储的材料。

重复周期

[编辑|编辑源代码]

当您按下学习, SuperMemo 会要求你重复（即审查应有的材料）。在每次重复中，您将执行以下操作：

  1. 查看问题并尝试回答它（在心里或口头上）
  2. 选择显示答案（在屏幕底部）查看正确答案
  3. 将您的回答（步骤 1 中的答案）与屏幕上显示的答案（步骤 2 中的答案）进行比较
  4. 选择一个等级这将反映您回答的准确性（例如好,失败等）（参见：等级描述）
  5. 选择下一次重复继续（并返回步骤 1）

重复周期可以更短。如果重复是被动的（例如阅读一篇文章），您可以立即跳到下一次重复阶段。

记住：

  • 您的第一次重复通常只会在您首次添加新材料后 3-5 天内出现。在它们到期之前，您只会看到“没什么可学的了”。这是正常的。如果学习不问任何问题，使用添加新的添加更多材料
  • 不要看计时器，在衡量您的表现时，响应时间会被忽略。
  • 如果你继续骑车经过项目而你永远不会看到“没什么可学的了“，你在最后演习阶段，您必须开始给出及格分数（好或太棒了）

定期重复是 SuperMemo 成功的关键

[编辑|编辑源代码]

在 SuperMemo 中，您首先记住材料，然后在程序要求您这样做时进行复习。

您按照自己的节奏记忆新材料，但一旦记住，您必须重复完全按照 SuperMemo 的要求。这不是程序的限制。这是人类记忆力的限制！

SuperMemo 的力量在于提供最佳时机重复。最理想的是，您应该每天使用 SuperMemo！这样你就不会积累优秀的材料并且您不会偏离最佳的重复时间表！

您应该每天进行重复，直到满足以下等效条件：

  • 您会看到以下消息：没什么可学的了
  • 杰出equals to0+0在统计数据窗户（或者简单地0）
  • 的第二个字段状态栏显示(0+0)/x，例如：(0+0)/23（或者简单地0）
  • 上的进度条状态栏变成蓝色（或处于非活动状态）

学习阶段

[编辑|编辑源代码]

每天，您的学习将分三个阶段进行：

  1. 重复次数- 复习之前记住的材料（以确保你不会忘记）
  2. Memorizing- 如果你有时间，你可以通过回答来记忆新材料是的到您想学习新材料吗？（此消息将被隐藏，直到您记住前 100 个元素）
  3. 最后演练- 复习在前两个学习阶段引起大多数问题的材料（跳过最后的练习必须取消签入工具包 :选项 :学习）

渐进学习

一旦掌握了上述基本学习流程，您就应该考虑学习渐进学习。

如上所述，经典 SuperMemo 与增量学习之间的主要区别：

  • 打字更少，学习速度更快
  • 阅读新文章或观看新视频可能会与标准重复交错（如上所述）
  • 的作用记忆和Final drill阶段减少了。所有材料均默认记忆，您的作品更容易记忆。这需要更少的钻孔
  • 在的帮助下优先队列，您可以添加更多的材料到您的收藏比你可能评论的要多。 This is why, in增量阅读，您通常还会有很多优先级较低的任务优秀的材料在一天结束时排队

由于增量学习相当复杂，您应该首先通过掌握本页所述的基本学习过程来感受 SuperMemo 的强大功能。
