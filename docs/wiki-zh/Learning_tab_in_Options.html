The Learning tab in Toolkit : Options provides options affecting the way you learn:

Interpretation of individual fields:

  • Forgetting index determines the default requested forgetting index. Forgetting index determines the speed of learning. When you first run SuperMemo, it is set to 10%. This means that SuperMemo will tune the learning process in such a way that you will stand a 10% chance of forgetting any given item at repetition. You can reduce the forgetting index to 3% at the cost of a significant slow-down of the learning process. You can also increase it up to 20% to greatly increase the speed of learning at the cost of knowledge retention. Increasing the forgetting index further makes little sense as both retention and the speed of learning will decrease (you can read about it in Theoretical aspects of SuperMemo or see it for yourself by using Toolkit : Statistics : Simulation). All individual items can have their individual forgetting index set with Edit : Edit parameters on the element menu (Ctrl+Shift+P). Read more: Forgetting index
  • Always randomize final drill - scramble the sequence of repetitions in the final drill before moving to the final drill stage
  • Skip final drill - do not create the final drill queue. Final drill must always be considered of lower priority than reviewing the outstanding material. For that reason, in a heavily overloaded incremental reading process you may come to a point where your learning sessions always end with Postpone (or start with Auto-postpone). If this is the case, building the final drill queue makes little sense
  • Midnight shift - the number of hours after midnight on which the new repetition day begins. For your convenience you should put the midnight-clock-shift value to the middle of your average night. For example, if your average sleeping hours are 23:00 - 7:00 (i.e. 11 p.m. - 7 a.m.), the middle of your night is 3 a.m. If you then set your midnight shift at 3 a.m. (slider at 3), you are least likely to be in a situation when you need to change the midnight clock shift to correctly execute repetitions belonging to a given waking day. If on occasion you come back home late at 2:30 a.m. and are still able to make repetitions, SuperMemo will behave like there was no date change at midnight. On the other hand, if you wake up at 3:30 a.m. and decide to make repetitions at that time, SuperMemo will already consider those repetitions as belonging to the new day. Midnight clock shift is extended to 16 hours for people in shift work and those who work in unusual hours (e.g. as a result of DSPS or ASPS sleep disorders). The change of date will not occur until you restart SuperMemo. For that reason, you should always close SuperMemo for the night.
  • Algorithm Arena Alerts - Triggers warnings when the interval calculated by Algorithm SM-20 significantly deviates from the value proposed by the legacy Algorithm SM-15.
    • Difference [%] - Raises an alert when the interval discrepancy (in percent) equals or exceeds this threshold.
    • Interval [days] - Sets a minimum interval length constraint. Discrepancies on intervals shorter than this value are ignored.
    • Factor - Raises an alert when the interval quotient compared to Algorithm SM-15 exceeds this limit.
    • Strict check - Apply strict parameters for interval comparisons. If different algorithms propose conflicting schedules, SuperMemo will frequently interrupt to let you manually select the optimum interval.
    • Relaxed check - Uses loose criteria so SuperMemo can automatically determine the optimum interval on its own. It does this by weighing the suggestions, heavily favoring the newer SM-19 and SM-20 algorithms.
    • Default check - Sets a safe, balanced baseline for comparisons. You can relax these settings later once you are comfortable with the interval weights SuperMemo picks for you.

各个字段的解释：

  • 遗忘指数确定默认值要求遗忘指数。遗忘指数决定了学习的速度。当您第一次运行 SuperMemo 时，它被设置为 10%。这意味着 SuperMemo 会调整学习过程，让你有 10% 的机会忘记任何给定的内容。项目在重复。您可以将遗忘指数降低到 3%，但代价是学习过程显着减慢。您还可以将其增加至 20%，以大幅提高学习速度以知识保留。进一步增加遗忘指数没有什么意义，因为记忆力和学习速度都会下降（您可以在SuperMemo 的理论方面或者使用以下命令亲自查看工具包 :统计数据 ：模拟）。全部个人项目可以设置他们个人的遗忘指数编辑 :编辑参数于元素菜单（Ctrl+Shift+P）。阅读更多：遗忘指数
  • 始终随机安排最终演练- 打乱顺序重复在最后演习在进入最后的演练阶段之前
  • 跳过最后的练习- 不要创建最终演练队列。最后演练必须始终被视为比审查优秀的材料。因此，在超负荷的渐进阅读过程中，您可能会遇到这样的情况：您的学习课程总是以以下内容结束：推迟（或从自动推迟）。如果是这种情况，则构建最终演练队列没有什么意义
  • 夜班- 新的重复日开始的午夜后的小时数。为了方便起见，您应该将午夜时钟转换值设置为平均夜间的午夜。例如，如果您的平均睡眠时间为 23:00 - 7:00（即晚上 11 点 - 早上 7 点），那么您的半夜就是凌晨 3 点。如果您随后将夜班设置为凌晨 3 点（滑块位于 3），则您最有可能遇到需要更改夜班以正确执行的情况重复属于特定的醒着的一天。如果有时您凌晨 2:30 很晚才回家，但仍然可以做事重复，SuperMemo 的行为就像午夜没有更改日期一样。另一方面，如果您在凌晨 3:30 起床并决定重复那时，SuperMemo 已经考虑了那些重复作为属于新的一天。对于轮班工作人员和非正常时间工作人员（例如，由于DSPS 或 ASPS 睡眠障碍）。在您重新启动 SuperMemo 之前，日期不会发生更改。因此，您应该在晚上始终关闭 SuperMemo。
  • 算法竞技场警报- 当以下情况时触发警告间隔由算法 SM-20 计算得出的值明显偏离传统算法 SM-15 提出的值。
    • 差异[%]- 当出现以下情况时发出警报间隔差异（百分比）等于或超过此阈值。
    • 间隔[天]- 设置最小值间隔长度约束。小于该值的间隔差异将被忽略。
    • 因素- 当出现以下情况时发出警报间隔与算法 SM-15 相比的商超出了此限制。
    • 严格把关- 应用严格的参数间隔比较。如果不同的算法提出冲突的时间表，SuperMemo 会频繁中断，让您手动选择最佳间隔。
    • 轻松检查- 使用宽松的标准，以便 SuperMemo 可以自动确定最佳间隔靠它自己。它通过权衡建议来实现这一点，并大力支持较新的 SM-19 和 SM-20 算法。
    • 默认检查- 设定安全、平衡的比较基线。当您对这些设置感到满意后，您可以稍后放松这些设置间隔SuperMemo 为您挑选的权重。
