Learn : Postpone can be used to move outstanding material review to a later time whenever you cannot cope with your daily load of repetitions. This option is particularly useful in heavily overloaded incremental reading. To fully understand the importance of Postpone, you need to master the concept of incremental reading first.

Why is Postpone important?

[edit | edit source]

Before the advent of incremental reading in SuperMemo, you had two extreme alternatives:

  1. traditional learning with high volume of the learning material and dismal retention. This learning can be called High In - High Out (HIHO) as high volumes of knowledge processed on the input are accompanied by equally high volumes of knowledge on the "output" (i.e. lost out to forgetting)
  2. SuperMemo with a small volume of strategic material remembered for years with a predetermined level of knowledge retention (LILO - Low knowledge input with low forgetting outflow)

Neither alternative provides a complete solution to the problem of forgetting. The balance between the two alternatives was left to the student. The optimum strategy was to use traditional learning to sift the strategic material, and then to memorize mission-critical material with SuperMemo.

The confinement of SuperMemo to strategic high-retention material was a powerful inhibitory force against SuperMemo's universal adoption:

  1. The process of selecting the strategic knowledge was not automated
  2. The student had to type in most of the learned knowledge to the program
  3. The student had to master knowledge structuring skills to make the memorized knowledge truly applicable
  4. Passive review was discounted as ineffective and was not used as a knowledge prioritization tool
  5. Retention was programmable solely via the forgetting index (within 3-20% forgetting rate)
  6. Accumulation of outstanding material after a short break was listed as the number one cause of users dropping SuperMemo for good

Incremental reading bridges the worlds of traditional learning and that of SuperMemo by providing a fluent transition between all priority levels starting from quick review, to repeated passive review, to active review, and to active recall at all levels of the forgetting index down to 3% (i.e. nearly 99% recall rate). Incremental reading also includes an anti-overload option: Postpone that blurs the concept of the must-do outstanding material that had levied a heavy toll on the ranks of SuperMemo users.

Before SuperMemo 2002, incremental reading did not automate the process of "prioritizing by overflow". In other words, high volumes of learning would force the student to manually reschedule learning in branches or subsets of lower priority. Manual rescheduling of the material was necessary to protect core knowledge from a dramatic drop in retention. Substantially reduced retention slows down the learning process not only via the forgetting index vs. acquisition rate relationship, but also through unraveling of the incremental reading process where the new knowledge is built on a shaky foundation of what has been learned earlier. In addition, the flow of thought in incrementally read articles becomes disrupted if the core knowledge retention is not protected.

SuperMemo 2002 introduced Postpone to make it possible to reschedule lower priority material with a keystroke. The student could now painstakingly work out priorities for individual subsets or branches of knowledge and then apply these easily on a daily basis.

SuperMemo 2006 dramatically simplified the process of handling material overflow by introducing the auto-postpone option (Learn : Postpone : Auto-postpone). With auto-postpone, you only need to define the priority of your elements. You can safely leave all postpone parameters in their default settings as these are now primarily used only to define the degree of postpone, which only defines the size of the pool of the low priority material used in interspersing the repetition queue without affecting the high priority material. In simple terms, you do not need to understand Postpone to use it effectively in incremental reading. Just leave Learn : Postpone : Auto-postpone checked. You can re-visit the subject if auto-postpone does not sufficiently reduce the workload in your collection.

Warning! Each time you use Postpone you depart from your chosen level of knowledge retention! Before you turn auto-postpone make sure that your material is well prioritized. Only then you will be sure that only low-priority elements suffer more forgetting.

To see the effects of rescheduling on the forgetting index see: Theoretical aspects of learning (forgetting index recovery figure).

Postpone dialog

[edit | edit source]

The Postpone dialog box presented below includes three tabs: Scope, Parameters, and Adjust.

Scope tab

[edit | edit source]

The Scope tab settings are mostly done automatically for you depending on the context in which you use Postpone. The Parameters tab makes it possible to define the degree to which items and/or topics are postponed in the selected subset, and which items and/or topics are exempt from rescheduling. Once you define Parameters you can save the new setting on the Scope tab with Settings : Save. Later you can restore the setting by using Settings : Name box.

Skip the following number of top priority elements determines how many elements should be left after the Postpone. If you choose 50, Postpone will first execute a standard postpone procedure, and then it will postpone more, if needed, until only 50 elements remain un-postponed. This option is useful in auto-postpone if you want to ensure that you inherit no more than a given number of outstanding elements from prior days of learning.

Figure: A typical use of Postpone in the browser: you can select the number of elements to remain outstanding (here 50), and click Postpone

Parameters tab

[edit | edit source]

You need to understand the Parameters tab if you want to customize the degree of delay in rescheduling.

Important! Some parameters must fall into a certain valid range. SuperMemo will correct those for you without warning. For example, if you set the minimum delay interval for items as 100 days, SuperMemo will change it to 30 days to make sure that you cannot force items to get a review chance at frequencies less than once per month. Naturally, other parameters permitting, less critical items might get delayed more (as determined by the Maximum interval for items).

Figure: Exemplary settings of Postpone parameters. Delay factor of 10 for topics means that topic intervals will be multiplied by 10, but will not be longer than 199 days, or shorter than 33 days. Intervals beyond 2500 days will qualify for a postpone skip (i.e. those elements will not be postponed, unless forced by the Method used on the Scope page). Postpone skip will also affect: items with the forgetting index less than 6, topics with A-Factors less than 1.01, elements that have been postponed more than 250 times, and items with priority greater than 3.5% (i.e. falling into the 0% to 3.5% range) and topics with priority greater than 0.0065%.

Delay factor determines how much elements should be delayed. For example, if you choose the delay of 1.1 (10%) on an element with the interval of 100 days, it will be delayed by ten days, i.e. rescheduled to the interval of 110 days. Postpone will always increase intervals by no less than one day from the present day. This way, all items on which Postpone is executed fall out of the outstanding subset.

Maximum interval puts a ceiling on the length of the delay interval. For example, if you choose the delay of 1.1 on an element with the interval of 200 days, and the maximum interval is 5 days, the element will be delayed only by 5 days (instead of the 20 days produced by multiplying the original interval by the delay factor).

Minimum interval puts a floor on the length of the delay interval. For example, if you set it to 3, all delayed elements will be delayed by no less than 3 days.

Skip conditions determine which elements will be exempt from delay.

  • if you check Skip items or Skip topics, items or topics (respectively) will not be rescheduled
  • Interval beyond will exempt elements with intervals longer than a selected value. For example, if you set Interval beyond to 320 days, no elements with interval longer than 320 days will be rescheduled. This can help you avoid infinite postpones. Once the element is postponed beyond a given interval, it will automatically enter the core of elements that will not be postponed
  • Forgetting index below makes it possible to determine the level of the forgetting index that will prevent items from being postponed. For example, if you set Forgetting index below to 5%, items with the forgetting index of 3% and 4% will not be rescheduled. This way you can make sure that the most important items in your collection are protected from a drop in retention
  • A-Factor below is analogous to Forgetting index below. The only difference is that you will use it with topics instead of items. Set A-Factor of most important articles to a very low value (e.g. 1.01). This way you will be able to filter them out at Postpone and make sure they never get rescheduled
  • Postpone count helps you prevent numerous postpones. For example, if you set the postpone count to 6 in the Items column, items will only be postponed 6 times. After that, Postpone will not affect them
  • Priority (%) makes it possible to avoid rescheduling of elements whose priority falls into a selected percentile bracket (low % indicates high priority)

Adjust tab

[edit | edit source]

The Adjust tab can be used for fine tuning delays on subsets with a complex priority structure of individual branches of knowledge. Sub-branch postpones determine how the rescheduling procedure interprets branches included in the subset that have their own postpone settings defined. Here are the possible options:

  • Respect settings - respect the setting defined for individual branches and ignore the currently used global setting of the Parameters tab. For a comprehensive postpone towards the end of your learning day (i.e. at times when you want to focus on your core knowledge), postpone all less important branches and be more lenient on branches that include most vital pieces of knowledge
  • Ignore settings - use the Parameters tab setting for all elements in the postponed subset. Ignore all branch postpone definitions. For example, if you want to postpone all topics in your collection, you can ignore the settings for individual branches, which may have Skip topics selected. This option is the fastest as it does not need to scan branches for individual postpone definitions
  • Always choose most conservative settings - check the branch setting and postpone as little as possible (i.e. choose the definition that is least drastic)
  • Always choose most liberal settings - check the branch setting and postpone as much as possible (i.e. choose the definition that provides for the highest delay). For example, minutes before you finish your learning day, postpone whatever can be postponed and focus only on the cream of the crop of your knowledge

Advanced settings on the Adjust tab include:

  • Include elements that are not outstanding for switching between Postpone and Dilute,
  • Modify item delay in proportion to the forgetting index for delaying items in proportion to the forgetting index,
  • Modify topic delay in proportion to A-Factor for delaying topics in proportion to their A-Factor, and
  • Modify delay in proportion to element priority for delaying elements in proportion to their priority.

Simulate, Postpone and Dilute

[edit | edit source]

Once you define the settings on the Parameters tab and, optionally, fine tune them on the Adjust tab, save them with Save on the Scope tab. You can now test the postpone procedure by clicking Simulate. If you are not satisfied with the results, redefine Parameters. Once you are happy with the result of the simulation, Save the settings and choose Postpone.

The option Dilute (e.g. Learning : Dilute on the subset processing menu) should only be used in rare circumstances. It is analogous to Postpone but it also delays elements that are not outstanding. There are two main reasons for using Dilute:

  • it may happen that you learn a lot of material in a branch that is no longer important to you. Instead of deleting such a branch, you may decide to incrementally sift it of the less relevant material. To reduce the emphasis on this branch in your daily review, use Dilute and spread the material over a longer period of time. Also use Priority : Decrease on the subset processing menu in the browser to make sure this low-priority material does not play an excessive role in your learning
  • if you transfer a portion of your learning process to another SuperMemo using XML export, you can Dilute the transferred material and continue learning on two independent platforms (e.g. pocket SuperMemo and SuperMemo for Windows). Dilute will prevent duplicating repetitions. See: Revolution in the pocket

Auto-Postpone

[edit | edit source]

Auto-postpone option is available with Learn : Postpone : Auto-postpone. It makes sure that excess repetitions are automatically postponed before the learning begins (if the option is checked).

Auto-postpone affects only outstanding elements scheduled on preceding days. For example, on Dec 1, 2009, repetitions from all days up to Nov 30, 2009 will be subject to default Postpone. Only repetitions scheduled on Dec 1, 2009 (and in the future) will not be affected.

To edit the default postpone settings, use View : Outstanding and Process browser> : Postpone on the browser menu. Skip the following number of top priority elements can be used to determine how many elements will be skipped when executing Auto-postpone. This field is independent of Skip conditions. If you set this field to 50, you will ensure that on any given day, only that day's repetitions and 50 unexecuted outstanding repetitions will be kept in the outstanding queue.

For example, if (1) Skip the following number of top priority elements is set to 50, and if (2) you have 600 outstanding repetitions from previous days, and if (3) you have 200 new outstanding repetitions, then 550 repetitions will be auto-postponed (600-50=550). This way, you will have 50+200=250 repetitions to make.

Using Postpone in various contexts

[edit | edit source]
  • globally - to postpone elements globally use Learn : Postpone : All elements
  • browser - to postpone elements listed in a browser, use Postpone on the subset processing menu (see also: Using subsets)
  • Contents window - to postpone elements in a given branch, select it in the Contents window, and use Postpone on the subset processing menu
  • element window - to postpone elements belonging to any ancestor branch of the current element (e.g. during repetitions), choose Learning : Postpone branch on the element menu. Respond with No until you reach the desired ancestor branch
  • Ancestors window (Shift+Ctrl+X) - right-click over any ancestor branch and choose Postpone branch

Examples

[edit | edit source]

If your daily load of items and topics keeps increasing, you can dilute the learning process with selective postpones from time to time. For example, you can opt to delay repetitions in subsets such as:

  • all articles and extracts
  • all newly added material
  • all material in a given knowledge branch (e.g. chemistry)
  • all difficult material
  • all short-interval material, etc.

Here are some typical ways in which you will execute Postpone:

  • to postpone topics, Learn : Postpone : Topics
  • to postpone all items (but not topics) in a given branch (e.g. all new items in your new items branch):
    1. select the branch in the Contents window
    2. choose View : Branch on the contents menu
    3. choose Child : Items on the browser menu
    4. choose Process browser> : Postpone on the browser menu
  • to postpone all repetitions in a branch to which a given element belongs:
    1. choose Learning : Postpone branch on the element menu when you see an element belonging to a lower priority branch. For example, when SuperMemo brings for review an item on the Bill of Rights, you might decide that your US Constitution branch should be postponed (e.g. because its priority is less than that of branches related to your profession)
    2. answer No until the desired parent branch is reached (SuperMemo will scan ancestor branches from the present one up to the root of the knowledge tree)

Postpone or Mercy?

[edit | edit source]

Postpone should drastically reduce the role of Mercy in SuperMemo. However, you may still need Mercy in the following circumstances:

  1. Randomizing repetitions (Toolkit : Mercy : Criteria : Randomize)
  2. Rescheduling the outstanding material after a longer break (with chosen Mercy : Criteria)
  3. Advancing future repetitions to an earlier date (e.g. before a vacation)

For more details see: Postpone, Advance and Mercy

为什么推迟很重要？

[编辑|编辑源代码]

在出现之前增量阅读在 SuperMemo 中，你有两种极端的选择：

  1. 传统学习学习材料量大且令人沮丧保留。这种学习可以称为高输入 - 高输出（HIHO），因为输入上处理的大量知识伴随着“输出”上同样大量的知识（即因遗忘而丢失）
  2. 超级备忘录少量的战略材料被记住多年预定的知识保留水平（LILO - 低知识输入和低遗忘流出）

这两种选择都无法提供遗忘问题的完整解决方案。两种选择之间的平衡留给了学生。最佳策略是使用传统学习来筛选战略材料，然后使用 SuperMemo 来记忆关键任务材料。

SuperMemo 仅限于战略性高保留材料，这是 SuperMemo 普遍采用的强大抑制力：

  1. 选择战略知识的过程不是自动化的
  2. 学生必须将大部分学到的知识输入到程序中
  3. 学生必须掌握知识结构技能让所记忆的知识真正能运用起来
  4. 被动审核被认为无效并且没有被用作知识优先级工具
  5. 保留率只能通过编程遗忘指数（遗忘率在3-20%以内）
  6. 累积优秀的材料短暂休息后被列为用户永久放弃 SuperMemo 的首要原因

增量阅读通过提供从快速复习到重复的所有优先级之间的流畅过渡，在传统学习和 SuperMemo 的世界之间架起桥梁被动审查, 到积极审查，并在各级主动召回遗忘指数降至 3%（即接近 99% 的召回率）。增量阅读还包括一个反过载选项：推迟这模糊了必须做的概念优秀的材料这对 SuperMemo 用户造成了沉重打击。

在 SuperMemo 2002 之前，增量阅读没有自动执行“按溢出优先级排序”的过程。换句话说，大量的学习将迫使学生手动重新安排学习时间分支机构或子集优先级较低。有必要手动重新安排材料，以防止核心知识急剧下降保留。大幅减少保留不仅通过遗忘指数与习得率关系，而且还通过解开增量阅读新知识建立在先前学到的知识基础上的过程。此外，如果核心知识不完整，那么增量阅读文章中的思维流程就会被打乱。保留不受保护。

SuperMemo 2002 推出推迟以便可以通过按键重新安排较低优先级的材料。学生现在可以努力地解决优先事项对于个人子集或分支机构知识，然后在日常工作中轻松应用这些知识。

SuperMemo 2006 通过引入自动推迟选项（学习 :推迟 ：自动推迟）。与自动推迟，你只需要定义优先级你的元素。你可以安全地离开一切推迟参数在它们的默认设置中，因为这些现在主要仅用于定义推迟程度，这仅定义用于散布重复队列的低优先级材料池的大小，而不影响高优先级材料。简单来说，你不需要理解推迟有效地使用它增量阅读。离开吧学习 :推迟 ：自动推迟检查过。您可以重新访问该主题，如果自动推迟并没有充分减少您的工作量收藏。

警告！每次使用时推迟你偏离了你所选择的水平知识保留！转弯之前自动推迟确保你的材料有很好的优先顺序。只有这样你才能确定只有低优先级的元素才会被更多的遗忘。

查看重新安排的影响遗忘指数参见：学习的理论方面（忘记索引恢复图）。

参数选项卡

[编辑|编辑源代码]

您需要了解参数如果您想自定义重新安排的延迟程度，请单击选项卡。

重要！有些参数必须落入某个有效范围。 SuperMemo 会在没有警告的情况下为您纠正这些错误。例如，如果您将项目的最小延迟间隔设置为 100 天，SuperMemo 会将其更改为 30 天，以确保您无法强制项目以低于每月一次的频率获得审核机会。当然，如果其他参数允许，不太重要的项目可能会延迟更多（由最大间隔对于物品）。

图：示例性设置推迟参数。延迟因素共 10 个主题意味着主题 间隔将乘以 10，但不会长于 199 天，也不会短于 33 天。间隔超过 2500 天将有资格推迟跳过（即那些元素不会被推迟，除非迫不得已方法用在适用范围页）。推迟跳过还会影响：项目与遗忘指数小于 6,主题与A 因素小于1.01，元素已被推迟超过 250 次，以及项目与优先级大于 3.5%（即落入 0% 至 3.5% 范围内）并且主题与优先级大于0.0065%。

延迟因素决定了多少元素应该推迟。例如，如果您选择延迟 1.1 (10%)元素与间隔100天的，将推迟十天，即改期到间隔110 天。推迟总会增加间隔从今天起至少一天。这样，所有项目其上推迟被执行掉出杰出的子集。

最大间隔对延迟间隔的长度设置了上限。例如，如果您选择延迟 1.1元素与间隔200天，最长间隔是 5 天，则元素仅延迟 5 天（而不是乘以原来的 20 天）间隔由延迟因子决定）。

最小间隔对延迟间隔的长度设置下限。例如设置为3，则全部延迟元素将延迟不少于3天。

跳过条件确定哪些要素将免于延误。

  • 如果你检查跳过项目或跳过主题,项目或主题（分别）不会重新安排
  • 间隔超出将豁免元素与间隔比选定的值长。例如，如果您设置间隔超出至 320 天，无元素与间隔超过320天将重新安排。这可以帮助您避免无限推迟。一旦元素被推迟超过给定的间隔，它会自动进入核心元素不会被推迟
  • 忘记下面的索引使得可以确定水平遗忘指数这将防止项目以免被推迟。例如，如果您设置忘记下面的索引至 5%，项目与遗忘指数3% 和 4% 将不会重新安排。这样您就可以确保最重要的项目在你的收藏受到保护，免受跌落影响保留
  • 下面的 A 因素类似于忘记下面的索引。唯一的区别是您将使用它主题而不是项目。套装A因素将最重要的文章设置为非常低的值（例如 1.01）。这样您就可以将它们过滤掉推迟并确保他们永远不会被重新安排
  • 推迟计数帮助您避免多次推迟。例如，如果您将推迟计数设置为 6项目专栏，项目只会推迟6次。之后，推迟不会影响他们
  • 优先级（%）使得可以避免重新安排其元素优先级落入选定的百分位数范围（低%表示高优先级）

  • 忽略设置- 使用参数所有人的选项卡设置元素在推迟的子集中。全部忽略分行推迟定义。例如，如果您想推迟所有主题在你的收藏，您可以忽略个别的设置分支机构，其中可能有跳过主题已选择。此选项是最快的，因为它不需要扫描分支机构对于个人推迟定义
  • 始终选择最保守的设置- 检查分行尽可能少地设置和推迟（即选择最不剧烈的定义）
  • 始终选择最自由的设置- 检查分行尽可能多地设置和推迟（即选择提供最高延迟的定义）。例如，在完成一天的学习之前几分钟，推迟任何可以推迟的事情，只专注于你知识中的精华

自动推迟

[编辑|编辑源代码]

自动推迟选项可用学习 :推迟 ：自动推迟。它确保在学习开始之前自动推迟过多的重复（如果选中该选项）。

自动推迟仅影响突出元素安排在前几天。例如，2009年12月1日，截至2009年11月30日所有日期的重复将被默认推迟。只有 2009 年 12 月 1 日（以及以后）安排的重复不会受到影响。

要编辑默认推迟设置，请使用查看 : 杰出和流程浏览器> :推迟于浏览器菜单。跳过以下数量的最高优先级元素可以用来确定有多少元素执行时会被跳过自动推迟。该字段独立于跳过条件。如果将此字段设置为 50，您将确保在任何给定的一天，只有当天的重复和 50 个未执行的未执行的重复将保留在未完成的队列。

例如，如果 (1)跳过以下数量的最高优先级元素设置为 50，如果 (2) 您前几天有 600 个未完成的重复，并且如果 (3) 您有 200 个新的未完成重复，则 550 个重复将自动推迟 (600-50=550)。这样，您将需要重复 50+200=250 次。
