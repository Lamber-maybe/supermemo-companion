  • SuperMemo 1.0 () was written in 1987, using Turbo Pascal 3.0. It was written by Piotr Wozniak. It was the first ever computer implementation of spaced repetition (for details see: Writing SuperMemo 1.0 for DOS).
  • SuperMemo 2.0 () written in 1988. It was released as shareware by SuperMemo World in 1991.
  • SuperMemo 3.0 () was the first major rewrite of the program to support DOS-type windows and menus.
  • SuperMemo 4.0 () introduced Algorithm SM-4 that would modify the function of optimum intervals to match student's performance.
  • SuperMemo 5.0 () introduced Algorithm SM-5. It was adapted for commercial distribution by SuperMemo World in 1991. It was released as shareware in 1993.
  • SuperMemo 6.0 () was the second major rewrite of SuperMemo, and the first version written for commercial distribution. It was the first SuperMemo to interlink learning with the student's forgetting curve. It was also the first SuperMemo to garner a number of awards (e.g. Software for Europe 1992). SuperMemo 6 was released as freeware in 1995.
  • SuperMemo 7.0 () was the third major rewrite of SuperMemo as the program changed platforms from MS DOS (Turbo Pascal 6.0 for DOS) to MS Windows (Turbo Pascal 1.0 for Windows). SuperMemo 7 was initially considered buggy and few users were willing to switch. It soon, however, added some multimedia (pictures and sound), and became one of the most beloved SuperMemos ever. It collected many awards and topped many popularity charts in Poland. SuperMemo 7 was released as freeware in 1999.
  • SuperMemo 8.0 () was the fourth major rewrite of SuperMemo (1994-1997). It was based on ideas of hypermedia SuperMemo included in Wozniak's PhD dissertation "Economics of Learning" supervised by Prof. Witold Abramowicz of the University of Economics in Poznan. Only a fraction of the original code survived the transition from Borland Pascal to Delphi 1.0. The program was such a severe departure from the original idea of SuperMemo that it had to hide behind a code name Genius for 3 long years. SuperMemo 8 was very slow in convincing users of SuperMemo 7 to make a transition. It was distributed primarily as a shell for multimedia courses for learning foreign languages in Poland. It was also the first SuperMemo to be sold as a download via the Internet, however, back in 1997-1998 it found only a handful of customers.
  • SuperMemo 9.0 aka SuperMemo 98 () was finally able to lure users of SuperMemo 7. Despite being a decade old, only now SuperMemo finally offered a rich text formatting capacity. It also offered a long-range of new multimedia options. It was also the only SuperMemo in history that sold over a million copies as Multimedia SuperMemo partwork (e.g. Easy English). SuperMemo 98 was released as freeware in May 2005. It also branched off Multimedia SuperMemo that was later rewritten from scratch as SuperMemo UX (currently used as a shell for multimedia DVD courses produced by SuperMemo World). SuperMemo 98 was the first SuperMemo to reach a truly international audience as it became available for download via the Yahoo! Store.
  • SuperMemo 10.0 aka SuperMemo 2000 () initiated a line of SuperMemo whose sole focus is incremental reading. The domain of multimedia was taken over by SuperMemo UX. Incremental reading was promoted under the banner "read thousands of articles in parallel", which naturally met with major skepticism. Fewer than 5% of users of SuperMemo 2000 tried incremental reading, and there were fewer than a dozen of true believers and practitioners in those early days.
  • SuperMemo 11.0 aka SuperMemo 2002 () gave incremental reading a significant boost by employing Internet Explorer to import articles from the Internet and for WYSIWYG processing of texts.
  • SuperMemo 12.0 aka SuperMemo 2004 () added an array of tools for use with incremental reading. It was also the first incremental reading software to be released as freeware in April 2010.
  • SuperMemo 13.0 aka SuperMemo 2006 () dramatically increased the power of incremental reading by adding a surprisingly simple concept: the priority queue. With the priority queue there is virtually no limit to the size of the learning material a student can tackle. High-priority material receives full protection from forgetting, while lower-priority material can still linger in the collection for opportune learning times. The priority queue also made it possible to add the now indispensable overload tools: auto-postpone and auto-sort. SuperMemo 2006 was the first SuperMemo to introduce a dedicated import filter for Wikipedia.
  • SuperMemo 14.0 aka SuperMemo 2008 () added YouTube-based incremental video as well as tools for optimizing sleep and learning (originally known from SleepChart freeware). SuperMemo 2008 also made huge progress expanding the set of tools needed in visual learning.
  • SuperMemo 15.0 () was the fifth major rewrite of SuperMemo (and first in 15 years). Now SuperMemo supports Unicode internally, which should make life easier for students of languages and for international users. As an extra bonus, SuperMemo code was simplified and made faster (esp. in processing texts).
  • SuperMemo 16.0 () was made even faster and more reliable than SuperMemo 15. It worked better in Windows 8 and with Internet Explorer 11. It integrated all incremental learning techniques. It required fewer clicks to access individual options. It added dozens of options for improving incremental reading, incremental video, incremental audio, and visual learning. It boasted the longest bug-fix list in history of SuperMemo (over 1000 significant fixes).
  • SuperMemo 17.0 () implemented a new repetition spacing algorithm: Algorithm SM-17. It shed a great deal of outdated functionality making it slimmer and faster. It also added concepts and neural review that help make learning more intelligent (e.g. when solving problems).
  • SuperMemo 18.0 () added 40 predefined themes (skins) to differentiate between collections and make learning more pleasurable. The interface was simplified. The algorithm was polished. Dozens of improvements focused on incremental reading, esp. the handling of concepts.
  • SuperMemo 19.0 () added Web import (starting with Microsoft Edge and Chrome). Dual monitor support (multi-monitor layouts) has been on the user wish-list for a decade. Most improvements in SuperMemo 19 focused on incremental reading.
  • SuperMemo 20.0 () added Algorithm Arena, PDF Editor for incremental reading, WebView component (e.g. for MathJax), and more
  • 超级备忘录 1.0（）于 1987 年使用 Turbo Pascal 3.0 编写。它是由彼得·沃兹尼亚克。这是有史以来第一个计算机实现间隔重复（详情请参阅：为 DOS 编写 SuperMemo 1.0）。
  • 超级备忘录2.0（）于 1988 年编写。它作为共享软件由超级记忆世界1991年。
  • 超级备忘录3.0（）是该程序的第一次重大重写，以支持 DOS 类型的窗口和菜单。
  • 超级备忘录 4.0（）介绍算法 SM-4这将修改最佳间隔的函数以匹配学生的表现。
  • 超级备忘录 5.0（）介绍算法 SM-5。它于 1991 年由 SuperMemo World 改编为商业发行。它于 1993 年作为共享软件发布。
  • 超级备忘录 6.0（）是 SuperMemo 的第二次重大重写，也是为商业发行而编写的第一个版本。这是第一个将学习与学生的学习联系起来的 SuperMemo遗忘曲线。这也是第一个获得认可的 SuperMemo奖项数量（例如 1992 年欧洲软件）。 SuperMemo 6 于 1995 年作为免费软件发布。
  • 超级备忘录 7.0（）是 SuperMemo 的第三次重大重写，该程序将平台从 MS DOS（Turbo Pascal 6.0 for DOS）更改为 MS Windows（Turbo Pascal 1.0 for Windows）。 SuperMemo 7 最初被认为存在缺陷，很少有用户愿意更换。然而，它很快就添加了一些多媒体（图片和声音），并成为有史以来最受欢迎的 SuperMemos 之一。收集了很多奖项并在波兰许多流行排行榜上名列前茅。 SuperMemo 7 于 1999 年作为免费软件发布。
  • 超级备忘录 8.0（）是 SuperMemo 的第四次重大重写（1994-1997）。它基于 Wozniak 博士论文中包含的超媒体 SuperMemo 的想法“学习经济学” 由波兹南经济大学 Witold Abramowicz 教授指导。从 Borland Pascal 到 Delphi 1.0 的过渡中，只有一小部分原始代码幸存下来。该程序与 SuperMemo 的最初想法严重背离，以至于它不得不以代号 Genius 隐藏了长达 3 年的时间。 SuperMemo 8 在说服 SuperMemo 7 用户进行过渡方面非常缓慢。它主要作为波兰学习外语的多媒体课程的外壳进行分发。它也是第一个通过互联网下载销售的 SuperMemo，但早在 1997 年至 1998 年，它只找到了少数客户。
  • 超级备忘录9.0又名超级备忘录 98（）终于能够吸引 SuperMemo 7 的用户。尽管已经有十年的历史，SuperMemo 直到现在才终于提供了丰富的文本格式化功能。它还提供了一系列新的多媒体选项。它也是历史上唯一一款作为多媒体 SuperMemo 部分作品（例如 Easy English）销量超过 100 万份的 SuperMemo。 SuperMemo 98 于 2005 年 5 月作为免费软件发布。它也是 Multimedia SuperMemo 的分支，后来从头开始重写为SuperMemo 用户体验（目前用作 SuperMemo World 制作的多媒体 DVD 课程的外壳）。 SuperMemo 98 是第一个真正吸引国际受众的 SuperMemo，因为它可以通过雅虎！商店。
  • 超级备忘录 10.0又名超级备忘录 2000（）发起了 SuperMemo 系列，其唯一关注点是增量阅读。多媒体领域被接管SuperMemo 用户体验。增量阅读打着宣传旗“并行阅读数千篇文章”，这自然遭到了极大的怀疑。不到 5% 的用户尝试过 SuperMemo 2000增量阅读，而早期真正的信徒和修行者不到十几个。
  • 超级备忘录 11.0又名超级备忘录 2002（）给了增量阅读显着推动使用 Internet Explorer 从 Internet 导入文章以及所见即所得的文本处理。
  • 超级备忘录 12.0又名超级备忘录 2004（）添加了一系列工具以供使用增量阅读。它也是第一个增量阅读软件，于 2010 年 4 月作为免费软件发布。
  • 超级备忘录 13.0又名超级备忘录 2006（）显着增加了功率增量阅读通过添加一个令人惊讶的简单概念：优先队列。随着优先队列学生可以处理的学习材料的大小实际上没有限制。高优先级材料得到充分保护，免遭遗忘，而低优先级材料仍可能在内存中徘徊。收藏以便有适当的学习时间。的优先队列还可以添加现在不可或缺的重载工具：自动推迟和自动排序。 SuperMemo 2006 是第一个引入维基百科专用导入过滤器。
  • 超级备忘录 14.0又名超级备忘录 2008（）补充说基于 YouTube 的增量视频以及优化睡眠和学习的工具（最初来自 SleepChart 免费软件）。 SuperMemo 2008 也取得了巨大进展，扩大了视觉学习所需的工具。
  • 超级备忘录 15.0（）是 SuperMemo 的第五次重大重写（也是 15 年来的第一次）。现在超级备忘录内部支持 Unicode，这应该会让语言学生和国际用户的生活变得更轻松。作为额外的好处，SuperMemo 代码得到了简化并且速度更快（尤其是在处理文本时）。
  • 超级备忘录 16.0（）比 SuperMemo 15 更快、更可靠。它在 Windows 8 和 Internet Explorer 11 中运行得更好。它集成了所有功能渐进学习技术。访问各个选项所需的点击次数更少。它添加了数十个改进选项增量阅读,增量视频,增量音频, 和视觉学习。它拥有 SuperMemo 历史上最长的错误修复列表（超过 1000 个重要修复）。
  • 超级备忘录 17.0（) 实现了一种新的重复间隔算法：算法 SM-17。它抛弃了大量过时的功能，使其更薄、更快。它还添加了概念和神经回顾有助于使学习变得更加智能（例如解决问题时）。
  • 超级备忘录 18.0（）添加了 40 个预定义主题（皮肤）来区分集合并使学习变得更加愉快。界面得到简化。该算法得到了完善。数十项改进集中于增量阅读，特别是。的处理概念。
  • 超级备忘录 19.0（）补充说网页导入（开始于微软边缘和铬）。十年来，双显示器支持（多显示器布局）一直是用户的愿望清单。 SuperMemo 19 的大部分改进集中在增量阅读。
