Algorithm Arena is a mechanism in SuperMemo 20 in which five spaced repetition algorithms compete in performance over the user's own memory data. The algorithm that best predicts recall earns greater weight in scheduling future repetitions. No single formula is trusted blindly. Performance on real data decides.

5 algorithms compete:

  • Algorithm SM-2
  • Algorithm SM-15
  • Algorithm SM-19
  • Algorithm SM-20
  • FSRS

Well-performing algorithms gain in weight. Defaults were determined by trial during the beta stage. The user's own balance usually develops in just 100–200 repetitions.

Superiority of the weighted pack over Algorithm SM-19 can be tracked via R-Metric in Statistics.

Individual indications can be spotted at discrepancy alerts during repetitions. Proposed values may show dramatic differences. The weighted interval proposition can be overwritten by the user.

Discrepancy alert frequency depends on the settings in Options. The greater the tolerance for discrepancies, the less frequent the alerts, and the more frequent the use of the arena proposition (weighted interval).

Recommendation: keep alerts frequent for educational purposes and use the Less often button in the algorithm alerts heatmap window to gradually reduce their frequency over time.

Important note: If you get conflicting information about the superiority of SM-19 or SM-20, here is a simple clarification:

  • SuperMemo SM-20 is a record holder on universal metric with metric approaching 0.0
  • SuperMemo SM-19 is often in the lead in the Algorithm Arena as SuperMemo SM-20 requires optimization via API that is at beta stage on release date (Apr 1, 2026). xxx

Figure: Only very long intervals magnify significant differences between various spaced repetition algorithms, incl. those that score very high on the universal metric benchmarks (e.g. SM-19 and SM-20). In the presented example, a very easy item from Advanced English achieved a very long interval of over 26 years in just 5 repetitions. The colored heatmap shows a weighted optimum with the darkest red color showing the best consensus of 32 years and 3 months. At the extreme ends of disagreement, Algorithm SM-2 points to an optimum of 66 years because it is unable to sufficiently reduce its E-Factor in just 5 repetitions (for successive grades 4 it would still imply 2.5-fold increase in interval). Unoptimized FSRS attempts to reconstruct the memory status using the three component model of memory and the history of repetitions. Each repetition adds to the potential error; however, its conservative 9-year interval proposition is a pretty safe bet that most users will still find as excessively long. In this particular case, optimized Algorithm SM-20 provided the closest bet to the weighted optimum of 32 years, but the actual "winner" will differ from repetition to repetition. Both FSRS and Algorithm SM-19 propose shortening the next interval; however, SuperMemo 20 disallows this (due to anti-cramming measures). The heatmap is a form of asymmetric bell-shaped distribution, and algorithmic weighing will receive microscopic nudges along the learning process depending on the R-metric scores. The black area on the left represents the previously used interval of 26 years. Important! Users can always override the recommendations of SuperMemo and set a shorter interval, e.g., with Ctrl+Shift+R


算法竞技场是一种机制超级备忘录 20其中五个间隔重复算法在性能上与用户自己的内存数据竞争。最好的预测算法回忆在安排未来方面获得更大的权重重复。没有一个公式是可以被盲目信任的。实际数据的性能决定。

5种算法竞争：

  • 算法 SM-2
  • 算法 SM-15
  • 算法 SM-19
  • 算法 SM-20
  • FSRS

性能良好的算法会增加权重。默认值是通过测试阶段的试验确定的。使用者通常只需重复 100-200 次即可提高自己的平衡能力。

加重包的优越性算法 SM-19可以通过跟踪R-公制在统计数据。

个别指示可以在差异警报中被发现重复。建议的值可能会显示出巨大的差异。加权区间命题可以由用户覆盖。

差异警报频率取决于中的设置选项。对差异的容忍度越大，警报的频率就越低，并且竞技场命题（加权间隔）的使用就越频繁。

建议：出于教育目的经常发出警报并使用不太频繁算法中的按钮会提醒热图窗口随着时间的推移逐渐降低其频率。

重要提示：如果您对 SM-19 或 SM-20 的优越性有相互矛盾的信息，这里有一个简单的说明：

  • SuperMemo SM-20 是一项记录保持者通用度量指标接近 0.0
  • SuperMemo SM-19 在以下方面经常处于领先地位算法竞技场因为 SuperMemo SM-20 需要通过 API 进行优化，该 API 在发布日期（2026 年 4 月 1 日）处于测试阶段。 xxx
